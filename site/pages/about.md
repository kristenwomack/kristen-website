# About Kristen

Hi, I'm Kristen 👋🏼

I'm an impact-driven product leader, technologist, and community builder. I'm a creator for makers and builders: developers, startups, and SMBs. I've been building APIs since 2008, and over 17 years I've led API platforms, developer programs, and product teams at companies ranging from scrappy startups to some of the most recognizable names in tech.

I build products and teams and have a track record of transforming complex, abstract goals and ideas into actionable steps, and inspiring teams with the enthusiasm to tackle them. I've been a natural leader since childhood, overflowing with ideas and recruiting others to bring them to life. I love nothing more than solving complex problems and bringing a vision to life. A natural ENTJ Myers-Briggs type indicator.

In my work life, this has translated into founding companies, building teams, launching products, leading departments (within 0 to 1 initiatives, scaling startups, and Fortune 100 companies), consulting, and serving as a board member and brand ambassador. My passion is building products and teams from the ground up, and leading with drive, vision, and fortitude.

## What I do now

I'm a Principal Product Manager at Microsoft leading the [Azure Developer CLI (azd)](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/developer/azure-developer-cli/), PM and one of the maintainers for the CLI for developer experience working with Azure, LLMs, and agents in software projects.

I serve on the Board of Trustees at [St. Catherine University](https://www.stkate.edu/), a higher education institution educating women to lead and influence. I'm an advisor to the board of directors at [Everyday Miracles](https://everyday-miracles.org/), an organization focused on birth equity. And I teach after-school intro to coding for 3rd to 8th graders.

## What I've built

At Best Buy, as Head of API Platform and Developer Program, I rebuilt the entire program from the ground up. I replaced an outsourced team of 100+ with a focused internal team of 23, saved $7.5M, launched 8 new products, and grew API key enrollment by 44%. That work included the first automated continuous delivery pipeline in the company's history.

At Microsoft, I led Developer Experience for the [Microsoft Graph API](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/graph/overview), one of the world's largest APIs, with 5,000+ endpoints and 20,000+ operations. I led the team of product managers and technical writers who built the SDKs, docs, tooling, and HTTP sandbox that developers use every day across Microsoft 365.

As a founder, I built three companies: [Hack the Gap](https://www.hackthegap.com/) (Minneapolis's first all-women hackathon, co-founded with Jenna Pederson, acquired 2019), Mina (a marketplace for parents and birth professionals), and Night Sky Web Co (a technology services company building technical solutions and prototypes).

The 3:30:3 Rule, a framework for API developer onboarding that maps the ideal time-to-first-call journey, shapes how I think about every developer platform I build. I've spoken about it at [Nordic APIs](https://nordicapis.com/) Platform Summit.
